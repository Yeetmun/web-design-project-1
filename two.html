Redundancy




Being able to see your stuff is all fine and dandy, but what if you have some extra important things that NEEDS to be seen? Or maybe you need to something clickable in your content such as a link to another resource or part of the site? That's alright let's just use different colors! How about we make our important things a nice looking red? This way, people using the site, or users will be able to quickly and easily see that it's something super important and click on it! We can test it out right here in this very sentence! Wait... where is the link?

Although the text already points you to where the link should be, it wasn't exactly easy to tell which word the link was, was it? Even though our original idea of using a standout color was theoretically a good one, the fact that many other words also had standout color meant that our color lost it's meaning. This can happen with any one method of giving emphasis to items such as bold or italics. Of course, whether or not a chosen method stands out is highly dependent on the content surrounding it, but with only one method, such as underlining, a user may not realize it's a link but instead just some word that you wanted to be emphasized, and not even attempt to click it.

The solution to this problem is to combine multiple methods while also making it quite clear that you're going to be providing something to click on, through explicit or implicit means. The standard <a> tag is actually quite a good example of this. When used without modifications it creates a piece of text that has a very identifiable blue color and an underline. Since text online will rarely ever be both blue and underlined, it will be much harder for users to misunderstand your content. Additionally, Even if your other content has different colors or is underlined, the combination of the two will ensure that your link still stands out in some way. Unless you write entirely in underlined blue, for some reason.

This general idea also applies to things like icons or buttons that want to capture the users attention and becomes especially important if your website features a lot of noise such as images or colors.
